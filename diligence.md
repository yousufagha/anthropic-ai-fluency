# Diligence

Notes from Anthropic's AI Fluency: Framework & Foundations course.

Diligence is the responsibility that comes after delegation and discernment: following through, documenting how AI was used, and being accountable for the final result.

## Key ideas

- Diligence is what turns "the AI did it" into "I did this, with AI assistance." The output carries my name, so the verification and judgment applied to it are my responsibility.
- Keep a record of significant AI-assisted decisions, especially where the AI's role could matter later, such as compliance, attribution, or a disputed claim.
- Diligence isn't about distrust of AI. It's the same due diligence I'd apply to any collaborator's work before putting my name on it.
- Diligence compounds with the other three skills: good delegation and description reduce how much diligence is needed, and good discernment is what diligence actually consists of applying.

## Practical takeaway

Before shipping AI-assisted work, ask: would I be comfortable explaining exactly how I checked this? If not, more diligence is needed.
