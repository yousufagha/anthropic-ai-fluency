# Discernment

Notes from Anthropic's AI Fluency: Framework & Foundations course.

Discernment is the skill of evaluating AI output critically instead of accepting it at face value.

## Key ideas

- Fluent output is not the same as correct output. AI systems can produce confident, well-formatted answers that are still wrong. The polish of the output says nothing about its accuracy.
- Verification effort should scale with stakes. A quick sanity check is fine for a low-stakes draft; a factual claim going into a report or a decision needs an actual source check.
- Know your own blind spots. It's easiest to miss errors in areas where I have the least expertise, which is often exactly where I'm relying on AI the most.
- Discernment includes noticing what's missing, not just what's wrong. A response can be accurate but incomplete in ways that matter.

## Practical takeaway

Treat AI output as a strong first draft from a capable but unaccountable collaborator, not as a finished, verified answer.
