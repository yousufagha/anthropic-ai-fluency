# Description

Notes from Anthropic's AI Fluency: Framework & Foundations course.

Description is the skill of communicating clearly enough that an AI system can actually do what you want. Vague instructions produce vague, or wrong, output.

## Key ideas

- Context does more work than instructions. What the AI knows about the goal, audience, and constraints usually matters more than the exact phrasing of the request.
- Specificity reduces rework. Naming the format, length, tone, and constraints up front saves more time overall than iterating after a bad first draft.
- Show, don't just tell. Examples of what "good" looks like, or what to avoid, are often more effective than abstract descriptions.
- Good description is iterative. The first prompt rarely nails it. Treating the exchange as a conversation rather than a single command gets better results faster.

## Practical takeaway

If the output isn't what I wanted, the fix is usually adding missing context, not just repeating the request more forcefully.
