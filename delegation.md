# Delegation

  Notes from Anthropic's AI Fluency: Framework & Foundations course.

  Delegation is the practice of deciding what to hand off to an AI system versus what to keep for a person to do. It's not just "can the AI do this," it's "should it, and under what conditions."

  ## Key ideas

  - Match the task to the tool. Not every task benefits from AI assistance — some are faster or safer done manually, and some require judgment or accountability that shouldn't be outsourced.
  - Consider the stakes. Low-stakes, easily-checked tasks (drafting, brainstorming, first passes) are good delegation candidates. High-stakes, hard-to-verify, or irreversible tasks need tighter human control.
  - Delegation is a spectrum, not a binary switch. You can delegate a sub-task, delegate with heavy oversight, or delegate fully and spot-check afterward. Choosing where on that spectrum to sit is itself a skill.
    - Delegating a task doesn't delegate the responsibility for it. Whoever hands off the work is still accountable for the outcome.

    ## Practical takeaway

    Before delegating, ask what could go wrong if the output is subtly wrong, and how easily that would be caught. The answer determines how much oversight the task actually needs.
